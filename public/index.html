Study tips from sensei
01Use Anki for vocabulary — spaced repetition is proven to stick words long-term.
02Never use romaji. Read everything in hiragana/katakana — you already know them!
03Morning session is for learning new content; evening session is strictly for review.
04Watch NHK Easy News daily — real Japanese at N5 speed with furigana.
05Write grammar examples in a notebook. Physical writing beats reading for retention.
06Use jlpt.jp official sample tests for mock practice — nothing else matches the real format.
